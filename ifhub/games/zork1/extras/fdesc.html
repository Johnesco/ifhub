FDESC: First Descriptions in Containers

A deep dive into ZIL’s FDESC property and why Inform 7 can’t replicate it for contained objects.

1. What is FDESC?

In ZIL (the Zork Implementation Language), every object can have an FDESC property — a “first description” that prints the first time a player encounters the object. After the player picks it up (or otherwise interacts with it), subsequent visits show the shorter LDESC instead.

FDESC serves a narrative purpose: it introduces objects with rich, atmospheric prose on first encounter, then steps back to brief, functional text on repeat visits. The egg isn’t just “a jewel-encrusted egg” — it’s a lovingly described treasure scavenged by a childless songbird. 

<OBJECT EGG
  (IN NEST)
  (FDESC
"In the bird's nest is a large egg encrusted with precious
jewels, apparently scavenged by a childless songbird. The egg
is covered with fine gold inlay, and ornamented in lapis lazuli
and mother-of-pearl. Unlike most eggs, this one is hinged and
closed with a delicate looking clasp. The egg appears
extremely fragile.")
  (LDESC "There is a jewel-encrusted egg here.")>

ZIL’s object printer checks a global TOUCHED flag: if the object has never been picked up, print FDESC; otherwise, print LDESC. This applies to all objects, regardless of whether they’re sitting in a room, inside a container, or on a supporter.

2. The Inform 7 Gap

Inform 7 has an equivalent concept: the "initial appearance" string (the text in quotes after an object declaration). This works perfectly for objects sitting directly in a room. 

The jewel-encrusted egg is in the bird's nest. "In the bird's
nest is a large egg encrusted with precious jewels..."

But here’s the problem: Inform 7’s initial appearance only fires for top-level room objects. If an object is inside a container (even an open one), Inform 7 silently suppresses its initial appearance and falls back to a generic contents listing:

ZIL (v0) >look
Up a Tree
You are about 10 feet above the ground nestled among some large branches. The nearest branch above you is above your reach.
Beside you on the branch is a small bird's nest.
In the bird's nest is a large egg encrusted with precious jewels, apparently scavenged by a childless songbird...
Inform 7 (before fix) >look
Up a Tree
You are about 10 feet above the ground nestled among some large branches. The nearest branch above you is above your reach.
Beside you on the branch is a small bird's nest. In the bird's nest is a jewel-encrusted egg.

The ZIL version prints the egg’s full four-line FDESC. The Inform 7 version prints a flat inventory-style listing. The atmospheric introduction is completely lost.

3. Case Studies

Case 1: Egg in Bird’s Nest (#79)

The bird’s nest sits in Up a Tree. Inside it is the jewel-encrusted egg — one of Zork’s most memorable treasures. On first visit, ZIL prints the egg’s full FDESC as a separate paragraph below the nest’s description. 

# ZIL source (dungeon.zil)
<OBJECT EGG
  (IN NEST)
  (FDESC
"In the bird's nest is a large egg encrusted with precious
jewels, apparently scavenged by a childless songbird. The egg
is covered with fine gold inlay, and ornamented in lapis
lazuli and mother-of-pearl. Unlike most eggs, this one is
hinged and closed with a delicate looking clasp. The egg
appears extremely fragile.")>

The fix: A Rule for writing a paragraph about the nest that manually prints the egg’s FDESC when the egg is present and unhandled, then marks it as mentioned so Inform 7 doesn’t also list it.

Rule for writing a paragraph about the bird's nest:
  say "Beside you on the branch is a small bird's nest.
    [line break]";
  if the jewel-encrusted egg is in the bird's nest and
    the jewel-encrusted egg is not handled:
    say "In the bird's nest is a large egg encrusted
      with precious jewels...[line break]";
    now the jewel-encrusted egg is mentioned.

Case 2: Canary in Egg (#81)

The egg contains a golden clockwork canary. If the thief opens the egg (which happens when you encounter him with the egg in your inventory), the canary’s FDESC should appear when you look at the opened egg on the ground. Two sub-issues:

  • FDESC suppression: Same container problem — the canary is inside the egg, so its initial appearance never fires.
  • Missing broken-canary text: The break-the-egg routine was silently swapping the golden canary for the broken one without printing the broken canary’s description text. In ZIL, the broken canary’s FDESC handles this automatically.
# ZIL: broken canary FDESC (dungeon.zil)
<OBJECT BROKEN-CANARY
  (FDESC
"There is a golden clockwork canary nestled in the egg.
It seems to have recently had a bad experience. The mountings
for its jewel-like eyes are empty, and its silver beak is
crumpled...")>

The fix: Two changes — a paragraph rule for the open egg that prints the canary FDESC, and an explicit say in the break-the-egg routine for the broken canary text.

Rule for writing a paragraph about the jewel-encrusted egg
  when the jewel-encrusted egg is open:
  if the golden clockwork canary is in the jewel-encrusted
    egg and the golden clockwork canary is not handled:
    say "There is a golden clockwork canary nestled in
      the egg...[line break]";
    now the golden clockwork canary is mentioned.

Case 3: Sceptre in Gold Coffin (#80)

The gold coffin in the Egypt Room contains a sceptre. When the player opens the coffin, ZIL prints the sceptre’s FDESC. In Inform 7, opening the coffin just said “You open the gold coffin, revealing a sceptre.” 

# ZIL source (dungeon.zil)
<OBJECT SCEPTRE
  (IN COFFIN)
  (FDESC
"A sceptre, possibly that of ancient Egypt itself, is in the
coffin. The sceptre is ornamented with colored enamel, and
tapers to a sharp point.")>

The fix: Two rules — an After opening rule for the reveal moment, and a paragraph rule for subsequent room descriptions.

After opening the gold coffin when the sceptre is in the gold
  coffin and the sceptre is not handled:
  say "The gold coffin opens.[line break]";
  say "A sceptre, possibly that of ancient Egypt itself, is
    in the coffin..." instead.

Rule for writing a paragraph about the gold coffin when the
  gold coffin is open:
  say "The solid-gold coffin used for the burial of Ramses II
    is here.[line break]";
  if the sceptre is in the gold coffin and the sceptre
    is not handled:
    say "A sceptre, possibly that of ancient Egypt itself,
      is in the coffin...[line break]";
    now the sceptre is mentioned.

Case 4: Thief Treasure Contents (#82)

When you kill the thief, the game lists all the treasures that reappear. In ZIL, this list includes the contents of open containers — “A gold coffin, with a sceptre”. The Inform 7 version just listed the containers without mentioning what was inside them.

The fix: A nested loop in the treasure listing that checks each open container for contents and appends them with natural-language formatting (“, with a sceptre” or “, with a canary, and a bauble”).

repeat with item running through things in the location:
  if the treasure-value of item > 0:
    say "[line break]  A [item]";
    if item is an open container and something is in item:
      say ", with ";
      [... enumerate contents with comma/and formatting ...]

4. The Solution Pattern

The general pattern for fixing FDESC in Inform 7 containers:

  1. Write a Rule for writing a paragraph about [container] that prints the container’s own description.
  2. Check for the contained object — if present and not handled, print its FDESC text.
  3. Mark it as mentioned so Inform 7’s standard contents listing doesn’t also print it.
  4. For reveal moments (opening a closed container), add an After opening rule that prints the FDESC instead of the default “revealing a [thing]” message.

Why “not handled” instead of a custom flag?

Inform 7’s built-in handled property tracks whether the player has ever picked up an object. This maps directly to ZIL’s TOUCHED flag that controls the FDESC/LDESC switch. Using the existing property avoids introducing redundant state.
